WORKING HOURS POLICY

Purpose:

The Company is committed to providing a work environment which is conducive to higher efficiency and increased productivity at the workplace.

The purpose of this policy is to establish uniform work norms (work timings, hours and days) for the staff. It has been designed to meet the needs of business operations as well as the needs of the staff.

Scope and Applicability:

This policy is applicable to all staff of the Company working at the HR Unit – ZIL Corporate Office, Gurugram (ZIL-CO.GGN).

This policy applies to all staff at the HR Unit – ZIL Corporate Office, Gurugram (ZIL-CO.GGN), and covers all Permanent Employees (PE), Full-Time Consultants (FTC), Trainees and Full-Time Contractual Employees (FTCE), deployed at the Unit.

This Policy does not apply to Part-Time Consultants (PTC), Part-Time Contractual Employees (PTCE) and Advisors, who follow contract-specific terms.

Policy

1. Working Norms: Work Week, Daily Working Hours and Work Timings

The Unit shall follow a five (05) day Work Week from Monday to Friday.

Standard Office Timing 9.00 AM to 6.00 PM
Flexible Entry Option 8.00 AM to 10:00 AM
Flexible Exit Option 5:00 PM to 7:00 PM
Lunch Break 30 minutes
Full Daily Working Hours 9 hours (mandatory)
Half-day Working Hours Minimum 4 hours and 15 minutes (excluding lunch)

In addition, late entry up to 10:30 am shall be condoned for a maximum of two (2) instances per month provided the staff has put in nine (9) hours of work on the same day(s).

2. General Attendance and Punctuality:

2.1. All staff are expected to report to work on time to maintain office decorum and discipline.

2.2. It is mandatory for all staff to mark their attendance whenever they enter or exit the office.

2.3. For all instances of late entry, consequences shall follow in the following order:

S.No. Action
1 Loss of Short Leave
2 Loss of half ( ) day Casual Leave (CL) after (1) is exhausted
3 Leave Without Pay (LWP), after (2) is exhausted

3. Attendance Procedure

3.1. First in-time and last out-time in biometric / electronic attendance system shall be considered for computation of Daily Working Hours.

3.2. In case the biometric/electronic attendance system does not recognize your attendance/ non-functional, the Manual Attendance Register shall be referred for attendance records.

3.3. Staff proceeding on official travel/meetings outside office must report as 'On Duty' and the same shall be approved by their Immediate Supervisors/Reporting Managers in the system in advance as far as possible.

3.4. Staff On Duty or On Deputation at another Unit shall follow the work hour timings of that Unit.

3.5. Payroll will be processed based on the attendance captured between 26th of the preceding month to 25th of the current month.

3.6. Payroll shall notify staff about absences/short working hours on 25th of every month.

3.7. Staff must apply for leave or request regularization of their attendance by 26th of every month regardless of whether they are On Leave or On Duty.

3.8. Immediate Supervisors/Reporting Managers must approve regularization requests by 5.00 PM on 27th of every month, regardless of whether it is a holiday.

4. Work from Home

Work from Home may be granted to staff in exceptional circumstances at complete discretion of the Management after due assessment and recommendation by the Group Head HR.

Policy Administration:

  • Corporate HR handles policy clarifications and interpretations.
  • Management reserves the right to revise this policy as needed.
