LEAVE AND HOLIDAYS POLICY

The Company is committed to fostering a work-life balance and recognizes leave as essential for rest, recuperation, and personal activities.

Purpose

This Policy establishes uniform norms for leave and holidays, balancing business needs with employee well-being while complying with regulatory requirements.

Scope and Applicability

This policy applies to all staff at the HR Unit - ZIL Corporate Office, Gurugram (ZIL-CO.GGN), and covers all Permanent Employees (PE), Full-Time Consultants (FTC), Trainees and Full-Time Contractual Employees (FTCE).

This Policy does not apply to Part-Time Consultants (PTC), Part-Time Contractual Employees (PTCE) and Advisors, who follow contract-specific terms.

POLICY

Leave Accounting Year

The Leave Year follows the Calendar Year: 1 January to 31 December.

I. Section A - Permanent Employees & Trainees

1. Privilege Leave (PL):

Eligibility and Entitlement:

  • 1.1. Twenty (20) days of PL per calendar year.

Carryover and Accumulation:

  • 1.2. Unused PL carries over, with a maximum accumulation of one hundred (100) days.
  • 1.3. Any PL exceeding one hundred (100) days as of 31 December 2024 will be parked separately and encashed in twelve (12) equal installments (April 2025 - March 2026) at the rate of Basic Pay as of 1 January 2025.

Clubbing & Availment:

  • 1.4. Can be clubbed only with Sick Leave (SL).
  • 1.5. May be prefixed and suffixed to both holidays or weekly offs.
  • 1.6. Intervening holidays/weekly offs are not counted as PL.
  • 1.7. Can be availed up to four (4) times in a Leave Year. (5th instance requires HR Head approval).
  • 1.8. More than ten (10) days requires approvals from the Reporting Manager and the Department Head/Business Head.
  • 1.9. PL accrues pro-rata from the date of joining (DOJ) and is available after six (6) months.
  • 1.10. Advance PL is not granted except in serious emergencies and is subject to recommendation of HRBP and approval of the MD.
  • 1.11. Half-day PL is not permitted.

Encashment:

  • 1.12. Encashment is at the Basic Pay rate.
  • 1.13. Auto Encashment: PL beyond one hundred (100) days is automatically encashed in January.
  • 1.14. Voluntary Encashment: Employees may encash up to twenty (20) days PL once in a year subject to maintaining a minimum PL balance of twenty (20) days.
  • 1.15. Unused PL at separation/superannuation will be encashed in the full and final settlement.

Other rules:

  • 1.16. Unauthorized absence/LWP leads to pro-rata reduction in accrual of PL.
  • 1.17. PL is granted at the discretion of the Company and must be requested at least a week in advance.
2. Sick Leave (SL):

Entitlement & Accumulation:

  • 2.1. Ten (10) days of SL per leave year, carried forward up to a maximum of sixty (60) days.

Clubbing & Availment:

  • 2.2. Can be clubbed with PL but not sandwiched between PLs.
  • 2.3. Cannot be clubbed with CL.
  • 2.4. Intervening holidays and weekly offs shall be counted as SL.
  • 2.5. Requires a medical certificate for absences of two (2) days or more.
  • 2.6. Half-day SL is not permitted.

Encashment:

  • 2.7. SL is encashed only at superannuation, total disability, or death, at the Basic Pay rate.

Prolonged Illness:

  • 2.8. Employees suffering from serious illness requiring prolonged absence and who have exhausted their SL, CL and PL balances may be granted unpaid Special Medical Leave, subject to Management approval.
3. Casual Leave (CL):

Entitlement & Usage:

  • 3.1. Seven (7) days per leave year, credited at the start of the Leave Year.
  • 3.2. Cannot be carried forward or encashed.
  • 3.3. Cannot be clubbed with any other leave.
  • 3.4. Maximum of three (3) days per instance.
  • 3.5. Prior approval is required except in emergencies.
  • 3.6. Half-day (½) CL is allowed.
4. Maternity Leave (MtL):
  • 4.1. Governed by the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (as amended).
5. Relocation Leave (RL):
  • 5.1. Three (3) days of paid Relocation Leave for transfers.
6. Leave Without Pay (LWP)
  • 6.1. May be granted under exceptional circumstances at Management discretion after exhausting other leave options.

II. Section B – FTC/ FTCE

Leave Types
  1. Earned Leave: Twenty (20) days for a twelve (12) month contract, earned monthly pro-rata
  2. Availment:
    • 2.1. Maximum five (5) days of EL may be availed at a time.
    • 2.2. Can be prefixed/suffixed to holidays or weekly offs.
    • 2.3. Intervening holidays/weekly offs are not counted as EL.
    • 2.4. No Encashment: Unused EL lapses at contract expiry.

III. Section C- Short Leave

  1. Available to Permanent Employees, Trainees, FTC, and FTCE
  2. Each Short Leave shall be of two (2) hours duration, can be availed twice a month.
  3. Must be taken either at the start (9:00-11:00 AM) or at the end (4:00 PM onwards) of the workday.
  4. Those availing Short Leave must complete at least seven (7) hours of work including lunch break.
  5. Two Short Leaves in a Leave Year shall result in half-day deduction in CL. If CL is exhausted, the half-day deduction shall be made from the PL balance of the employee. In case of no CL or PL balance, two Short Leaves would be treated as day LWP (Leave Without Pay).

GENERAL LEAVE RULES & GUIDELINES

Leave during Notice Period:

  1. Only three (3) days of SL is permitted.
  2. Other leaves may be granted only subject to discretion of Management and shall result in extension of the notice period by an equal number of days.
  3. Unused PL is encashed in the full & final settlement.

General Guidelines

  1. Leave applications must be submitted via the HR Portal.
  2. Prior approval is mandatory unless warranted by an emergency.
  3. Those applying for Half-day leave must put in at least four (4) hours, fifteen (15) minutes of work (excluding lunch).

Public Holidays:

  1. Ten (10) public holidays annually, including compulsory national holidays.
  2. One (1) optional restricted holiday (RH) per employee.

Policy Administration:

  • Corporate HR handles policy clarifications and interpretations.
  • Management reserves the right to revise this policy as needed.
